Saxo

An expressive therapy app

Process

  • Research: user interviews, affinity map, storyboard, persona, empathy map
  • Test prototype & revise
  • Visual Design: mood board, style guide, animations, & prototype

Problem Statement

Therapy sessions can be expensive, how might we provide the benefits of expressive therapy in the comfort of your own space for free?

User research

I surveyed 10 people and interviewed 3 people, who enjoy doing some form of art for leisure. And also learn about people’s relationship with mental health.

Learned about depression & anxiety cycle, and art therapy helps people struggling with depression.

Art heals, but only when it's non-competitive, done in private, in the moment.

I organized the research findings in an affinity map

Brainstorming

‘Body introspection’, a phrase one interviewee used stucked in my mind. They spoke of the joy from dancing on their own.

I decided to explore the relationship between movement and music.

Storyboard of someone improvising music for their mindfulness activity

Constraints

How can I prototype & test an interaction that relies on sound and motion in 2 days?

I lacked motion sensor chips and Arduino while living abroad

The sounds made must sound good, no matter how the user moves

But all I need to gauge people’s emotion response to the act of generating music by moving around.



We can fake it!

My Android phone had an accelerometer and gyroscope!

I download an obscure song. It will play when the device is in motion, and paused when still.

Testing

Learnings

Using an unknown song did not fool people into thinking that they were improvising music.

Nonetheless some users expressed the remixing popular songs would be just as fun as making new songs.

Revisions

If want to make an improv music app, there will be many untested wireframes still left to design.

I decided to pivot and market it as a conducting music practice to help people struggling with anxiety feel in more control.

Iceberg of thoughts and feelings from someone about to have a panic attack

After revising a few wireframes, it's time to move on to visual design

The UI of this project is inspired by electric glows & spotify's May 4th easter egg.

Establish a style guide

User adds songs to their bundle to remix

Remix song by moving their arms like a conductor.

Afterwards we check in with how they are feeling
